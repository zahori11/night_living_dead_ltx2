# 📝 Script Notebook: Video Prompts and Art Direction

This document centralizes all text instructions sent to the AI (Gemma) to generate the radio studio sequence. The main objective is to maintain absolute consistency in the **1-bit pixel art** style throughout all shots.

---

## 🛡️ 1. Base Parameters and Core Style

Unlike the action (which changes in each shot), these parameters establish the **visual foundations** of the film. They define the engine and the base style, accounting for exceptions when on-screen graphics appear.

### 🟥 The Negative Shield (And Its Variations)

The **Negative Prompt** acts as a barrier against smoothing, but **adapts** depending on whether news graphics (banners) appear on screen.

**A. Standard Shield (Shots without on-screen text):**
> (anti-aliasing:1.5), (soft gradients:1.2), (soft lighting:1.2), (blurry:1.2), (soft edges:1.2), undefined details, muddy, smooth faces, blurry eyes, featureless skin, blurred details, wax figure, out-of-focus face, body movement, swaying, breathing motion, torso twisting, 3D render, hyperrealistic, photography, additional colors, modern video, watermarks, ugly, deformed, artifacts, glitches, bad anatomy

**B. Banner Shield (Shots with news text):**
*(Director’s Note: In scenes where the announcer shows signs such as "YOUNGSTOWN" or "SHARON", we use this variation of the negative prompt to allow the AI to draw clear letters without interpreting them as "watermarks" or "artifacts").*
> (anti-aliasing:1.5), (soft gradients:1.2), (soft lighting:1.2), (blurry:1.2), (soft edges:1.2), undefined details, muddy, smooth faces, blurry eyes, featureless skin, blurred details, wax figure, out-of-focus face, body movement, swaying, breathing motion, torso twisting, 3D render, hyperrealistic, photography, additional colors, modern video, ugly, deformed, bad anatomy
